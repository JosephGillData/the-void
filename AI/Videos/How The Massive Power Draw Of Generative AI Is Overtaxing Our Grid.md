This CNBC documentary examines the intense strain that the rapid growth of generative AI is placing on global power grids and water supplies. As data centers expand to meet the massive computational demands of AI models, their energy and cooling requirements are scaling at an unsustainable rate.

Key points from the discussion include:

- **Escalating Energy Needs:** The energy consumption of data centers is rising significantly each year. Training and running large AI models require vastly more power than traditional internet tasks, leading to higher greenhouse gas emissions and pushing utility companies to keep aging power plants online longer than planned.
- **Grid Fragility:** Existing electrical infrastructure is struggling to transmit the necessary power to these high-demand clusters. To cope, some data center providers are implementing load-shedding programs or even building their own localized power plants to avoid overloading the public grid.
- **Water Usage Concerns:** Beyond electricity, cooling massive server farms consumes enormous amounts of water, leading to regional environmental concerns and public backlash in several countries. This is driving a shift toward more sustainable, water-less cooling methods and liquid-to-chip technologies.
- **Efficiency and Innovation:** Industry leaders are focusing on "more work per watt" as a primary solution. This involves transitioning to more energy-efficient, Arm-based processors and exploring on-device AI processing to reduce the total load on massive, centralized data centers. These innovations are seen as critical steps to sustaining the growth of AI technology without overwhelming current resource constraints.
