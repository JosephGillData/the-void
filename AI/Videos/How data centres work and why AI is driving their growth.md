## Summary

- Data centres are **physical facilities that store, process, and transmit data**
- AI is rapidly increasing demand for:
    - Compute (GPUs)
    - Power
    - Cooling
- This is driving a global **infrastructure expansion**

---

## Key Ideas

### 1. Data Centres Are Physical Infrastructure

- Not abstract “cloud”
- Real buildings filled with:
    - Servers
    - Networking equipment
    - Power systems
    - Cooling systems

---

### 2. Core System = Power → Compute → Heat → Cooling

- Electricity powers servers
- Servers perform computation
- Almost all energy becomes heat
- Cooling systems remove that heat

---

### 3. AI Is Changing Everything

- Traditional workloads = moderate compute
- AI workloads (especially training):
    - Extremely power-intensive
    - Require specialised GPUs
    - Generate much more heat

---

### 4. Power Is the Primary Constraint

- Data centres require massive electricity supply
- New AI data centres:
    - Consume power comparable to small cities
- Location depends heavily on:
    - Grid capacity
    - Energy cost

---

### 5. Cooling Is Becoming a Bottleneck

- GPUs produce high heat density
- Air cooling is often insufficient at scale
- Shift toward:
    - Liquid cooling
    - More advanced thermal management

---

### 6. Data Centre Growth Is Accelerating

- AI demand → rapid expansion of infrastructure
- Hyperscalers (e.g. Amazon Web Services, Microsoft, Google):
    - Investing heavily in new facilities
- Increasing competition for:
    - Land
    - Energy
    - Hardware (GPUs)

---

## Details

### Why Location Matters

- Data centres are often built near:
    - Cheap electricity
    - Renewable energy sources
    - Cooler climates (reduces cooling cost)

---

### Infrastructure Layers Highlighted

- Physical:
    - Buildings, racks, cables
- Power:
    - Grid, backup systems
- Cooling:
    - Airflow or liquid systems
- Compute:
    - CPUs, GPUs, storage

---

### AI-Specific Pressure

- Training large models:
    - Requires thousands of GPUs
    - Runs continuously for days/weeks
- This creates:
    - Sustained high power draw
    - Continuous heat generation

---

## Actions

### What to take away (for your learning)

- Reconfirm your core model:
    - Data centre = **energy + heat management system**
- Connect to your plan:
    - Power → Cooling → Layout → Strategy
- Use this question:
    - “If AI demand doubles, what breaks first?”

---

## Bottom Line

- AI is not just a software shift
- It is driving a **physical infrastructure revolution**
- The real constraint is no longer algorithms
- It is **power, cooling, and compute at scale**
