### What are Data Centres
In the 1950s-1980s, companies had one central location with big computers (mainframes), where all the data processing occurred. Literally, the data (processing) centres. 
The name data centre has stuck to today, but they now do so much more. They are really:
* Compute centres, GPUs for AI and CPUs for normal compute tasks
* Storage centres: databases and files
* Network hub: routing global traffic

### Why Data Centres are Important
Data centres run the worlds IT infrastructure, including:
* The cloud: websites, apps, SaaS tools, storage
* AI: training models and creating predictions
* Everything else: enterprise IT systems, government databases, financial infrastructure, telecom backends
The core of this infrastructure are data centres. Without these working, nothing works. Therefore, data centres are critical.
