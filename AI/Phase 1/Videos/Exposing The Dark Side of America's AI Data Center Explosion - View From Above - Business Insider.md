This video investigates the rapid and often secretive expansion of artificial intelligence data centers across the _United States_. As AI technology grows, tech giants like _Amazon_, _Google_, _Microsoft_, and _Meta_ are constructing massive server farms at an unprecedented rate, yet there has been no official federal record of their locations or environmental impact until now.

**Key environmental and social concerns include:**

- **Water Consumption:** Many data centers are situated in drought-stricken regions, such as _Arizona_, where they consume millions of gallons of water daily for cooling, often competing with local agricultural and residential needs.
- **Power Demands:** The massive electricity requirements of these facilities are straining the power grid. To keep up, some utility companies are delaying the retirement of coal and natural gas plants, hindering progress on carbon emission targets. In some cases, massive infrastructure upgrades are required, the costs of which may eventually be passed on to residential energy consumers.
- **Residential Impact:** Many data centers are built near residential neighborhoods. Local residents report significant distress due to the constant, 24/7 low-frequency drone emitted by cooling systems and the visual impact of massive, industrial-scale buildings in suburban settings.
- **Lack of Transparency:** Big tech companies frequently utilize trade secret exemptions and non-disclosure agreements to prevent the public from accessing data regarding their energy usage, water consumption, and total footprint.

Ultimately, the report highlights a tension between the world's growing appetite for cloud computing and AI versus the tangible resource, health, and financial costs borne by the communities hosting these industrial server farms.
