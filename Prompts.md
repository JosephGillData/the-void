
## Obsidian
### Conversation Summary
``` promt
Summarise this entire conversation into a structured knowledge node.  
  
Output format:  
  
TITLE:  
(A short, specific, descriptive title for this topic. Max 8 words.)  
  
SUMMARY:  
(A clear, concise explanation of the core idea in 150–250 words.)  
  
KEY POINTS:  
- Bullet point list of the most important insights  
- Keep each point short and atomic  
  
DETAILS:  
- Expand on any important technical or nuanced ideas  
- Include examples if relevant  
  
NEXT STEPS:  
- Actionable things to explore, build, or refine next  
  
TAGS:  
(5–10 keywords for categorisation)

```

### Structure a lesson on a subject
```
Act as an expert AI infrastructure tutor.

I am learning about data centre fundamentals as part of a structured 2-week plan.

Today’s topic is: [INSERT TOPIC]
Example: "Power and redundancy" or "Cooling systems"

Your job is to guide me through ONE focused learning session (~45–60 minutes).

Structure the session exactly like this:

1. Concept Overview (Simple)
- Explain the topic in plain English
- No jargon unless necessary
- Use clear mental models

2. How It Works (Step-by-Step)
- Walk through what actually happens in the real world
- Make it practical, not theoretical

3. Why It Matters
- Explain why this is a constraint or important in data centres
- Link to AI workloads where relevant

4. Failure / Stress Scenario
- Describe what happens when this system fails
- Make it realistic and concrete

5. Key Trade-offs
- List the main trade-offs (cost, scale, reliability, etc.)

6. 5 Key Insights
- Give 5 sharp bullet points I should remember

7. Questions for Me
- Ask me 3–5 questions to test my understanding

8. Optional: Visualisation Guidance
- Suggest what I should search on YouTube to see this visually

Keep it concise, structured, and focused on understanding — not memorisation.
```
